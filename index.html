The starting point

You already listen. The tools just listen slowly.

Most organisations run several listening channels, and each has the same two problems: the signal arrives late, and people soften what they say. The result is a picture of how your people felt months ago, filtered through what felt safe to write down.

Annual engagement surveyOne snapshot a year, results a quarter later
Pulse survey emailsSurvey fatigue, falling response rates
One-to-onesHonest, sometimes, with one person
Suggestion boxA black hole with a slot in the top
Exit interviewsThe truth, finally, on the way out
HRIS & absence dataRecords what happened, not how it felt
The white space: nothing gives you an honest, live picture of how work feels today, in each team, with a loop that visibly closes: people speak, leaders act, and everyone sees what changed.
How it works

One closed loop, built on trust

Four steps, one shared data model, three views of the same live picture. All from tapping a simple device placed where your people already are: break rooms, entrances, canteens, staff exits.

1
CaptureA colleague taps a Loop device. A webapp opens with their location already known. A mood takes one tap; an issue, an idea, a shout-out or an end-of-shift check-out takes a few seconds. No app store, no login, no tracking of who tapped.
2
SeeLeaders see a live pulse by team, the themes behind it, and anonymous voices in colleagues' own words. Updated in seconds, seen daily, instead of an engagement report read once a year.
3
ActAlerts carry duration and the number of colleagues affected, so pressure is visible while it can still be fixed. Leaders choose how to respond, and a person shapes every response.
4
CloseAction posts to a You said / we did wall every colleague can see. Where someone chose to share their name, a confidential reply goes to them alone. Trust is earned in this step, and measured.
↺ and every closed loop makes the next voice more honest
🔒
The foundation: anonymity your people can verify, not just believe.

Anonymous is the default, not an option buried in settings. Leaders see themes and team scores, never names. An enforced anonymity floor means nothing displays for any group under five responses, so a small team can never be worked out. A colleague's name enters the system only when they switch it on themselves, to ask for a confidential reply or a private conversation. From one tap, your people can:

Check in, anonymously Raise an issue Recognise a colleague Share an idea Ask for support

The path to real

Working software, ready to shape around you

What you just used is a working build, not a slide. The production system persists data, manages permissions, and is deliberately integration-first: it sits alongside your HRIS and engagement platform rather than replacing them, and feeds both. It can be customised for your organisation's needs, your language, your team structures and the platforms you already have. Governance is designed in from the start: the anonymity floor, data minimisation, GDPR and works-council requirements are architecture, not an afterthought.
